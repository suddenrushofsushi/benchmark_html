Best ever almond and coconut milk!

Leave a reply

I’ve been searching high and low for almond milk  that a) isn’t too thin and watery, b) actually tastes good, and c) isn’t ridiculously expensive ($10 a litre?! c’mon!!)

The thought of making my own vaguely appealed to me, but it seemed a bit complicated, and trying other people’s samples seemed they had the same problem with watery consistency and general lack of flavour. However, I recently found this great recipe:

http://blogs.kcrw.com/goodfood/2013/04/recipe-the-holy-grail-of-homemade-almond-milk/

Although oats are not ok for scd, I thought the idea to add the coconut was a pretty rad one, so I gave it a whirl with some modifications. I’m not at all ashamed to say this is the most delicious almond milk I’ve ever tried! So thick, so creamy, so much flavour! I haven’t tried steaming it, but I think because it’s quite thick it should steam well and so will be awesome for coffee, hot chocolate etc.

Best ever almond and coconut milk

You’ll need:

-1 cup of almonds

-1 cup of coconut (flaked, desiccated, whatever you like)

-3 cups of water- boiled, then left for about 5 minutes

-A small handful of walnuts

-1 teaspoon of cinnamon

 

Method:

1. Put your almonds, coconut and walnuts into the food processor, and add 1.5 to 2 cups of hot water. Blend well.

2. Once blended, add the additional 1 to 1.5 cups of hot water and blend again.

3. Rest for half an hour.

4. Push mixture a small batch at a time through a strainer or cheesecloth, pressing hard to get all the liquid out.

5. Collect the dry pulp into a container to use later- it’s great for baking.

6. Add the cinnamon on top of your warm milk and put it into the fridge to cool. If it’s too thick for your liking, add a little water, stir, and continue to add water until you’re satisfied with it.

 

Depending on your pressing skills, you should have around 250 ml (8.5 oz) which should keep for 4-5 days in the fridge.

Mushroom and leek tart- SCD, vegan, GF

Leave a reply

It’s the first day of winter here in Australia! One of my favourite things about winter is all the delicious warming winter veggies- pumpkin, carrots, beetroots, cauliflowers and leeks. Leeks seem to get a bit of a bad rap here although I’m not sure why- they are so versatile, so delicious, and so easy to cook!

This delicious tart is a cinch to whip up with only a couple of ingredients, and the creaminess of the leeks contrasts so well with the crunchiness of the almond base. This dish works as easily as a quick midweek meal or as an impressive starter to serve to guests.

Mushroom and leek tart

For the almond base you’ll need:

1.5 cups of almonds

A generous knob of butter (vegan butter if vegan- I use Nuttelex Lite for this recipe)

1 egg or equivalent in egg replacer (I used Bob’s Red Mill egg replacer)

For the tart topping you’ll need:

1 large leek

5 button mushrooms

1 tablespoon of butter (again, vegan butter if you’re making it vegan)

Salt and pepper, to taste

Method:

1. Preheat oven to 180°C (356 F)

2. Blend almonds, butter, egg/egg replacer in a food processor until the almonds are chopped but still a little chunky.

3. Line an 8-inch square baking pan with baking (parchment) paper and place almond mixture on top, pressing it down firmly.

4. Bake until it turns golden brown on the top (about 15 mins) then remove from the oven and rest aside.

5. While the base is cooking, roughly chop the leeks into 1.5 cm (0.5 inch) circles, using the white and the light green bits, but not the dark green stems. Chop mushrooms into smallish chunks.

6. Heat a frypan over moderate-high heat, and add the butter/vegan butter. When sizzling, add your leeks and stir frequently.

7. When leeks are translucent and beginning to caramelise, add mushrooms and continue to stir frequently until softened.

8. Add salt and pepper to taste.

9. Remove the almond base from pan, and spoon leek and mushroom mixture over the top. Serve it while it’s hot, or let it cool- it’s just as delicious either way!

Sumac, salt and chilli silverbeet (chard) chips

Leave a reply

So by now I guess everyone’s across kale chips, right? So delicious, SO much healthier than greasy old potato chips, and perfect for the scd diet. But, have you ever tried silverbeet chips? Silverbeet (or chard, depending on where you’re from) has larger, slightly thicker and flatter leaves than kale, and somehow absorbs the flavours more easily than kale making it so much more amenable to delicious spice combinations.

My most recent favourite flavour combination is sumac, salt and chilli- the tanginess and the little kick from the chilli just compliment the crispy silverbeet so well!

To make these little gems, you’ll need:

  • 1 bunch of silverbeet
  • Olive or canola oil – I find spray oil works best, but you can use bottled oil too.

About 1 teaspoon per tray each of:

  • Salt
  • Sumac
  • Chilli

Method:

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C (338 F)
  2. Rip your silverbeet leaves off the large stem on either side and then tear again into smaller pieces. Remember that they will shrink, so make them a bit larger- about the size of your palm is a good rule of thumb.
  3. Rinse under running water, and shake dry.
  4. Line baking tray(s) with baking (parchment) paper
  5. Arrange silverbeet on baking paper- don’t worry if they overlap a little, but try not to layer them or they won’t cook well.
  6. If using whole chillies, chop finely and discard the seeds (or keep them if you like things a bit spicier). Store-bought chilli flakes also work just as well.
  7. Spray your silverbeet with oil so they are covered, but not soaked. Same applies if you’re using bottled oil- use sparingly, otherwise they won’t crisp up.
  8. Sprinkle silverbeet evenly with salt, sumac and chilli.
  9. When oven has reached the set temperature, pop the silverbeet in.
  10. After ~8 minutes, remove from the oven and flip each piece over. If your silverbeet still looks a little limp, put it back in the oven for another 2-3 mins before turning each piece.
  11. Bake for a further 4-5 minutes
  12. Remove from oven. They cool down really quickly so are ready to each almost straight away.

Enjoy!!

Welcome!

Leave a reply

Hiya, thanks for visiting my blog!

I started on the specific carbohydrate diet (scd) about 5 months ago, and while it’s been fantastic so far for my health, I found that many of the scd recipes around online were not all that reproducible, required copious expensive ingredients, or were overly focused on desserts. Added to that, I don’t eat any meat or dairy, and my housemate follows a vegan diet, so we found ourselves mostly winging it and making up our own recipes. We often get asked about the foods we are eating, how we made it, what we substitute, etc., so I decided to start this blog to document the everyday things we’re cooking.

The focus of this blog will be easy, everyday scd legal, vegan and gluten free meals that can be made quickly and with minimum fuss. Everything posted here will be trialled by me before posting, so you know it’s legit!

About me:

I’m Sarah, a 28 year old neuroscientist from Melbourne, Australia.

Happy eating! :)
