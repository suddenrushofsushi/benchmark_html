My Yellow Balloon {Holiday Gift Guide}

November 2, 2014 By momentsinmommyland 2 Comments

Have you ever read a book with a deeper meaning then you originally perceived? Has a story ever give you the shivers or brought tears to your eyes? That’s what happened to me last night while reading My Yellow Balloon by Tiffany Papageorge.

A boy named Joey goes to a carnival with his parents and receives a beautiful gift, a bright yellow balloon. He becomes very attached, playing with it, sleeping with it and taking it everywhere he goes. But one day his balloon disappears, and Joey isn’t sure how to deal. He’s angry, confused and doesn’t know how to handle the emotional impact of his friend being gone.

My Yellow Balloon touches on a sensitive and important topic for little children – how to deal with loss and grief. Whether it’s a grandparent, friend or pet, the book puts the concept into a simple format they can understand and relate too, without being overly upsetting.  I truly loved this book and my son was captivated by the images of the boy and his balloon.

The illustrations by Erwin Madrid are beautiful, which isn’t surprising considering his talent has been seen in films such as Shrek and Madagascar. And though this is the authors first published work, she has spent years working with teachers and mental health professionals helping children cope with loss.

I hope schools and families take a book such as this into their collection. Grief is a hard subject, but stories like this can help us all heal.
