How to Be More Low-Maintenance

01/10/2014   Beauty, Minimalist   9 Comments

I think a lot of us stress out about our appearance and try to cut down on how long it takes us to get out of the door. To truly live simply and minimalist as a woman can be difficult at times. So how can you be more low-maintenance to make your day-to-day life simpler?

Work with your hair, not against it.

Avoid hip haircuts like pixies and bobs that require monthly visits for trimming or cuts that require daily straightening. Talk to a stylist about your hair type and ask them to give you something you can both grow out long-term and wake up and go with.

For example, instead of straightening curly hair on a daily basis, get a cut suited for curly hair and embrace it. Or if you have straight to wavy hair like me, grow it out to weigh it down. Want to color hair? Ask for a slight ombre going from dark to light, allowing you to grow it out much longer without that harsh hairline showing.

While it’s always fun to dress up, the long-term goal should be as little interference with your hair as possible on most days!

Invest in your skincare, not your make-up.

The better your skin, the less make-up (if any) that you’ll have to wear. Invest in natural products for your skin and make sure it’s properly hydrated. Go days without putting anything on.

I’ve gotten to the point where I just do eyebrows and some blush and it saves a ton of time. You’ll start getting compliments on how great your skin is instead of your make-up, and isn’t that a much more flattering compliment?

Again, it’s fun to dress up. But on most days, you should be able to take 5-10 minutes on your looks and run out the door.

Keep nails in check and forget about them.

If you’re always fretting about breaking nails and keeping them all the same length, either cut them down or invest in gels. Gels are more of an investment price-wise but can be the solution for weak nails that constantly require upkeep or polish that’s always chipping. You can even paint over gels and forget about them for another week or two.

Sometimes you have to way pros and cons of your work and personal situation to find the right solution that will allow you to spend less time worrying about your appearance.

Have a wardrobe with style, not abundance.

Go through and clean out your closet. Only keep things that speak to you, and you’ll end up getting ready at lightning speed.

Too many of us keep clothes we’re not crazy about, hoping we’ll like it later. As it turns out, all that does is waste our time trying it on in the future, only to put it back on its hanger. Donate those pieces and trade it for something stunning to your body type! It also helps to plan outfits the night before — you’ll be ready in no time.
