Monday, June 15, 2015

Birthday and Graduation Parties

We just had a very fun event to celebrate, as you know.  We celebrated Penny's graduation on May 16th!
 
 
 
But, we waited to have the party, later, when more of my (Chandra's) relatives could make it.﻿
We had a very busy and fun graduation party weekend starting with my parents and one brother arriving to help us get ready for the party, which was held on June 6th.  Here are some pictures of everyone getting ready.  First of all, my dad helped by replacing the passenger seat in my car with one he found for me at a salvage dealer, since the seat broke back at Christmas time.



 
He's such a handyman!  He also fixed our downstairs toilet, which was great for all of our out-of-town guests who were staying at our house!  We also had another man come and fix our leaky sunporch roof just days before the parties.
 

 
Also, our very kind, former nextdoor neighbor graciously gave us some of her lawn furniture, since she sold her house and moved to a smaller apartment.  That was very nice to have for all of the extra company!
 
 
One of the things she gave me was an old beverage cart.  I just spruced it up by cleaning it and covering up the old, rusty surface by spray painitng it with Rustoleum Metallic spray paint.  It fit perfectly in the corner of our sunporch, where I was planning on serving our punch for the party!
 
 
Unfortunately, I never took a picture of the punch, which was very delcious!  I loved the idea I got from pinterest to freeze the ice in the bottom of a liter bottle, so that it comes out in the shape of a flower.  And I added blueberries to the ice, so they'd go into the punch, as the ice melted.  It was a real hit with lots of people going back for more.  The punch recipe was very easy.  It had frozen o.j., canned pineapple juice, banana blended in and 7-Up.  We made it up ahead of time and kept it in gallon jugs in coolers of ice, so that it would be ready to pour into the punch bowl on the day of the party.﻿
 

 
﻿
 Then, we celebrated Penny's 20th birthday with our relatives and Penny's friend from HSE.
 






 
I baked some of the cake for the graduation party ahead of time.  But, then I had to go back to work.  So, my mom finished baking the rest of the cake and helped out with decorating it.  We love how it turned out!
 




 
It looks like Grandpa couldn't wait to try some of the cake!
 
My brother, Brian, and Penny helped out a lot with decorating for the party.  Here they are hanging balloons and streamers.
 
 
The day of the party, they also had to hang up many bows that had fallen down overnight.
 
 
So, here was the garage, all decorated and ready for the guests to arrive...
 

 
It was so nice of our neighbors to let us use their helium tank to blow up some balloons!
 
And here was the cake, just waiting to be cut up and eaten by the guests....
 
 
And here is the lovely graduate with her cake, before it gets all cut up and eaten.
 



 
Then, she was quickly back outside to play with Brian and watch them set up the badminton set in the front yard, as well as the croquet set in the backyard.
 



 
Then our guests began arriving.  We were so glad that they all got along so well and seemed to enjoy the taco bar, punch, cake and ice cream and other food that we served.  I made my own seasonings for the beef and chicken taco meat, so that it would be gluten-free.  That way all of my guests were able to eat the food that we served.  Well, except for the cake, of course.  Sorry, Mom!  You got to make it, but never taste it.  Fortunately, Penny and her friends seemed to really enjoy playing lots of games together.











 
 We were so glad that we could spend such a fun time with our friends and relatives, celebrating Penny's graduation!  And we are so proud of her many accomplishments by completing her high school education.  It's been a very fun adventure, especially since we home schooled and got to share in the journey together.
 

 
It was a great party and the fun just continued into the next days and week.  But, that will be the next post!  Thanks for visiting our party!﻿

Wednesday, May 20, 2015

Happy Graduation 2015!

Well, our lovely daughter who has only been a part of our family for six and a half years has now officially graduated from high school.

 
She's still been finishing up some of her school work.  Here she is studying for her final exam in Health, with her cozy study partner.
 
 
I'm glad that Penny has made a good friend through Home School Etc.  Here are a few pictures of her with her friend from Graduation and the party that followed it the next day.
 


 
  Penny graduated along with twenty-five other seniors who were also part of Home School Etc.  That is almost three times the number of kids I graduated with.  Yes, my graduating class was only 9, including myself!  Here is Penny's "big" class...
 
We were pleased that her dress finally arrived in the mail two days before the ceremony!
 
 
  She was a lovely graduate! It was so nice to have her Grandma Dottie, Uncle Rick and Aunt Doreen, as well as our friends the Kremers and our pastor with us to celebrate!  And now I've finished my painting project in the sunporch to get ready for the party that is coming up in June.
 


 
It was a great weekend to celebrate!  Hope yours was glorious, too!  And hope you all have a safe Memorial Day!
