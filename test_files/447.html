Wellness Wednesday


Made this wonderful recipe tonight! The grating process was a little time consuming but it was worth it!!!

Ingredients
5 medium sweet potato
1/2 cup diced frozen onions
2 tbsp Olive Oil
6 eggs
1 tbsp Mrs Dash original blend



1. Preheat oven to 350.
2. Grate your sweet potato with a cheese grater OR if your food processor has a grating feature use that. It will make life SO much easier.
3. Heat up a frying pan on high heat. Add EVOO. Add the sweet potato, onion and Mrs Dash. Cook until sweet potato begins to brown and soften.
4. Add sweet potato hash in 9 x 13 greased pan. Begin to create holes for your eggs.
5. Crack your eggs into the hash and bake for 15-20 mins depending on how runny you want your eggs.
6. Sprinkle with desired seasoning and serve.

Chicken Cabbage Stir-Fry
- 3 chicken breast halves
- 1 large bag of frozen stir fry veggies
- 3 cups green cabbage, shredded
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- ½ tablespoon garlic
- ½ teaspoon ground ginger
-1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
- ½ cup water
- 2 tablespoons organic liquid aminos (soy sauce substitute)

Cut chicken breasts into strips. Heat oil in frying pan, saut chicken. After chicken is finished add cabbage and cook until tender. Boil water for frozen stir fry veggies (follow directions on the bag). Add in cooked stir fry veggies. Mix seasonings, organic liquid aminos and water together and then pour over the mixture and simmer for 5 minutes.



This is a perfect pre-workout snack or a nice way to kick a sweet tooth!

Banana Blueberry Baked Oatmeal

2 cups rolled oats
1 tsp baking powder
1/2 tablespoon cinnamon
1/4 cup honey
2 cups Almond milk
2 eggs
1/2 cup unsweetened apple sauce
2 tsp vanilla extract
2 bananas, sliced into 1/2 inch pieces
2 cups blueberries, fresh or frozen, divided

Preheat oven to 375 degrees. In a medium bowl, mix together oats, baking powder and cinnamon. In second mixing bowl, combine honey, almond milk, eggs, apple sauce and vanilla. Spray Olive Oil cooking spray on 8 x 8 baking dish. Arrange bananas and half of blueberries on the bottom. Cover with dry oat mixture. Pour liquid mixture on top of dry mixture, making sure its completely covered. Sprinkle remaining blueberries on top. Bake for 35 minutes or until set. Let cool before cutting. Serve hot or cold, topped with a little more almond milk!
