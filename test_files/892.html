School spirit in the makeup aisle: a frugal beauty option

Posted on August 20th, 2013 in Fashion and beauty, Football, Just for fun, Makeup, Newspaper column, Pinterest

The Monroe on a Budget column runs Tuesdays in The Monroe Evening News. Here is this weekâ€™s installment:

School spirit for your face and nails on football game days is possible to create for less money than you might think.

All you need to do is go to the makeup aisle of any drugstore or supermarket and look for your team colors among the product lines offered by companies such as Cover Girl, Maybelline and Revlon.

Nail and eye colors can be found today in any shade imaginable including emerald green, bright blue, gold and steel gray. Even lipsticks can be found in orange or purple, not just red and pink.

To further personalize your designs, you can purchase nail art pens or stencils, or nail art jewels, in the makeup aisles.

And here’s the money-saving tip: if your selections are from everyday makeup selections, you can use everyday retail promotions such as manufacturer coupons, bundle-up sales and drugstore rebates to cut the cost.

Another reason to pick your colors from regular merchandise lines is that many of the makeup kits that are packaged and promoted for game day are only “one time” uses.

As I was researching this column, I kept a lookout for what I could wear on game days. I expect to attend some football parties this season for my alma mater, University of South Carolina, with an alumni chapter in Detroit.

The nail shades I picked out at Rite Aid to match my college colors of garnet and black are Sugar Sugar (dark red) and Black on Black from Sinful Colors’ Rock It collection. I paid only $1.99 for each bottle.

While black isn’t a business professional color, that is certainly not a one-off selection for my makeup box! After all, colors from a Rock It collection would be perfect to wear to the Monroe County Jam on Saturday Aug. 24.

I also picked out the Kiss nail art kit at Walgreens. This cost $6.99, but was a bargain in my book. There were small bottles of white, black and silver nail colors with 30 reusable sticker stencil patterns.

Another frugal tip is to make an appointment at Michigan College of Beauty in Monroe to have someone do your nails. Prices for salon services are very reasonable, as the student technicians need clients to practice on. For example, a regular manicure is $6, and a French manicure is $10.

Beyond the color selections, CollegeFashion.net has a great list of tips for “Game Day Beauty” that include putting your hair up in braids or ponytails, and using waterproof mascara.

For design inspiration, take a look at the inexpensive and easy-do nail art and face makeup designs I pinned at pinterest.com/monroenews/game-day-style.
