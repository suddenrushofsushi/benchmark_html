Dollar Store Linen Closet Makeover

Well, hello there...
I'm back from my little holiday bloggy break!

We had a nice Sort Of Fairytale Christmas this year!
Maggie, who is 2, really got into this year with her love of all things 
Frosty, Rudolph, and Santa.
Ahhh, Christmas really is for kids isn't it?

For me, it all went by in a flash...the baking, shopping, visiting, hustle and bustle of it all. 
 I don't really mind when it ends either, so I can get back to usual routines and such.  
 I like routine, what can I say?  
Much like my love of routine, I also love organization...I dream of a super organized well run, well thought out house.  I know...I'm a real party animal like that! 

The new year always brings magazine covers with "Get Organized", "Get Healthy" headlines, January is the month of reinvention it would seem.  If you can't beat 'em, join 'em I say!

I started my linen closet makeover before Christmas actually, but didn't officially finish it up until this morning.  I have to say, I am so pleased with it and sometimes open the door for no other reason than to just to look in there and oooh and ahhh at the orderliness.  
Oh, and this was all done with items from the dollar store and cost around $20!


 Here's a horrifying before shot...

 and another one...

well, that was embarrassing, let's look at the glorious after again!


ahhhh, much better.

 All the bins and baskets came from the dollar store and lucky for me they came in bright, happy colors!  Although, I do think natural baskets would have been nice too, but much more money, and I have more important vintage goodies things to spend my money on.


I plan on filling those spray bottles {pictured above} with natural cleaning recipes soon, so stay tuned for that...it will either be the greatest thing ever, or utter disaster!

The baskets got labeled with crafty items I had on hand.  I used my price tag paper punch and card stock paper for the label, and tied it on with coordinating ribbon.


I feel so much more organized now in this area of my home, dare I say, possibly reinvented??

What I think I am most proud of though is creating an organized, yet pretty closet for hardly any money!  I love a good thrifty challenge...that's how I party these days.

XO,
Stacey
