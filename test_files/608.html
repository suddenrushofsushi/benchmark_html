How To Put Together a Gift Basket on a Budget

By Casey | December 2, 2013 7 Comments
  • Tweet
  • Pin It

**I am a member of the Collective Bias/Social Fabric Community. This shop has been compensated as a part of a social shopper insights study for Collective Bias and their client.

Please tell me I’m not the only one who absolutely loves the episode of F.R.I.E.N.D.S where Joey and Chandler need to buy Christmas presents for the gang at a gas station after getting into one of their many shenanigans. This has always been hilarious to me because for so long I really dismissed buying legitimate gifts for people from gas stations, drugstores, etc because I just sort of assumed that they wouldn’t offer anything of substance. Let me just tell you – drugstores have stepped their game way up! After browsing the new Walgreens Holiday Gift Guide I just knew that I could put together a gift basket that would be perfect as a last minute {and affordable} gift for a hostess, a gift for your child’s teacher, or really anyone! In that episode of F.R.I.E.N.D.S it was painfully obvious that Joey & Chandler picked up their gifts in an act of last minute desperation but it doesn’t have to be that way! You can totally put together a quality gift basket at Walgreens and I’m going to show you how!

I noticed that there were a ton of tasty snack suggestions so that’s what I based my inspiration off of because really, who doesn’t love to indulge in a bit chocolate from time to time? I chose to hit up the Walgreens in the Empire State Building since I know they have a great selection of pretty much everything and I was immediately taken aback (in a good way!) by the selection of Good & Delish products. I don’t know why I’ve never paid more attention to this brand (which is the Walgreens store brand in case you were confused like I was, haha) but their selection seriously rocks. They have everything from coffee to gourmet appetizers to party snacks and even ice cream! After browsing for a bit I decided to stick to non-perishable snacks so I beelined for the cookie and candy aisle to begin my project.

I seriously wanted to buy every single item I could get my hands on because not only did they look delicious but the prices were awesome as well. Walgreens was also having some pretty sweet sales so you know I capitalized on that.

Putting together this gift basket was pretty simple! I tried to get a nice mix of products that would appeal to a variety of people so I made sure to add different flavors of chocolate to mix it up a little bit! I included everything from mint to caramel with sea salt as well as a mix of both milk chocolate and dark chocolate. There was definitely no shortage of options!

I saw this little snowman mug and just had to have it – I’m obsessed with his little face! After seeing that the Good & Delish tea was on sale for $2.49 a box I figured that this would be a nice little personal touch to add to the gift basket. I also purchased some Christmas tissue paper for $0.99 to help the items stand up in the basket while still looking festive. I then added some ribbon to the front to dress it up a little bit. I think little touches like this really help take gift baskets up a notch from the place of “I picked this up last minute on my way to your house.”

The items in my gift basket only cost $30 and most snack items were less than $3.00 each! Can you believe that?! With my Balance Rewards card I saved $8.18 (14%!) so I used those savings to buy things like the tissue paper, ribbon, and a basket to help tie in everything.

Overall this was a really fun project and I’m loving all the things I included! What do you do when you need a last minute gift for someone? Have you ever been so desperate that you’ve bought presents at a gas station like Joey & Chandler had to? Comment below and let me know!

Until next time
