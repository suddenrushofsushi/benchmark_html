That's 70's

3/23/2015

0 Comments

 
Hi everyone, welcome back! Fashion month is over and the trends for the season are set! One of my favorite trends that took over the runway was the 70's. This look is fully inspired from a bunch of my favorite shows throughout fashion month. The 70's were all about embracing new looks and trying different things. Denim and shearling were all the rage, as they are again now! I hope you like how I do the 70's, give or take 50 years later. (I'm also debuting my short hair cut that I am obsessed with and I plan on chopping the rest off at Easter time!!) 
DRESS- TOPSHOP
JACKET- BURBERRY 
SHOES- NINE WEST
PURSE- CHANEL 
The 70's are back people!! I picked up this dress a few months back and have been wearing it constantly. I've loved using denim in my outfits lately. I don't typically wear dresses, but I have worn this dress over a pair of blue jeans and black jeans, but I love how the dress stands out in this look too. Pairing the dress with socks and heels is also something that was seen on multiple runways this past month. I have been wanting to try this sock and heel combo since the middle of fashion month and I was finally able to try it with this outfit. I also think this combination is perfect for the weather right now too as it is a cute alternative to tights. 
Shearling jackets were the biggest trend on the runways. Almost every designer showed their take on the updated shearling coat. My jacket is clearly not shearling on the outside like the traditional ones, however it is lined on the inside. This jacket screams modern 70's to me and I absolutely love it. I also loved how the length of the jacket and the dress added a bit of dimension to the look and gave me a shape. 
This look is by far one of my favorites. I have a feeling I will be loving the 70's for a little while longer! Who knows, maybe I'll even dive into the back of my closet and find my old bell bottoms... but we won't get ahead of ourselves. 


Simply,

With a Twist....

2/17/2015

1 Comment

 
Hey guys! I hope everyone is enjoying their Tuesday! I feel like I haven't been on here in so long, so I thought I would catch you guys up on the last few weeks! I last left you with the news of my collaboration with Vernez. Shortly after that, I got an email from Tom Ford asking if I was interested in going to their offices for an interview for the PR internship position that was available. I went on several interviews over Christmas break including Tom Ford and Christian Louboutin. Long story short, I was offered the internship position at Tom Ford and I chose to accept the offer. I chose this offer over any other for multiple reason, however the main one being that I simply felt at home at TF. 
I work three full days a week at their corporate offices on Madison Ave as their PR intern. Tom Ford is an experience unlike any other that I have had. Internships that I have had in the past have taught me a lot and I am very thankful for them, but at Tom Ford I feel apart of the close knit family that Mr. Ford has created. The company is extremely small, as TF likes to keep his employees close and comfortable. TF as a brand is one that I have admired for a few years now and being able to actually be apart of the success first hand is something I am so proud of. The atmosphere is fast paced, focused, lighthearted, genuine, and hilarious. There is never a dull moment in the office and showroom (which is where I work). 
I am so proud of myself and grateful for this incredible experience and I can't wait to continue my time working at TF! 
School is also in full swing so between work, school, and finishing up getting the apartment perfect, I have been one busy girl. 
BUT I have a new post for you all today and I hope you guys like it!  Since it is Fashion Week here in NYC I thought I would show you a look I wore! ENJOY!! 

DRESS- Forever21
LONG BLAZER- Forever21
JEANS- H&M (DIY knee rips)
PONCHO- Burberry 
SHOES- Michael Kors
HAT- H&M
BAG- Celine
This look is a favorite of mine. I found this dress at Forever21 as I was rushing through the store with my brother and we both stopped in our tracks and he said "thats you" right as I was going to grab my size. Its a mid calf tight cotton graphic dress. A few posts back I mentioned how I don't love graphic pieces but this one was so fun and subtle. It says Warner Bros Studio in gold, followed by a long description in English and Japanese in white. I paired this dress with my favorite black jeans. I am really into the ripped knee look with jeans, even though they are fading out of trend. (Look out the 70s are making their comeback!!!) I paired these two together and kept the hem of the dress right above the knee cut outs. I do love black on black outfits but this is my twist on that trend. Since everyone is obsessed with black on black right now, even though its quickly fading out, I think it is still fun to try different things with it...like pairing a long dress and jeans. 
I picked up this long blazer at Forever21 as well. I love the slate grey color, since I do wear neutrals on a daily basis I thought this would go great with the majority of my closet. I am only 5'3 so the reason I can pull off such a long blazer is because it has two high slits on either side. The slits show off my legs when I walk so I don't look shorter than I already am. My favorite part of this look was the length at which the dress hits and the slightly longer length of the blazer. It looked oddly symmetrical and cool. 
Its also extremely cold and snowy in Manhattan (I actually came home to surprise my parents for the night which is why I am in Connecticut in the photos, but I was in Manhattan during the morning and afternoon time). In order not to freeze I threw on my Burberry poncho to bring a little depth to the outfit and for some color. I also am obsessed with this piece so I make any excuse I can to wear it with almost everything! I also wore my 'go-to' black ankle boots because there is enough snow in NYC and Connecticut to last a lifetime.
The amount of people who stopped me in Manhattan and then back in Connecticut to say how much they loved this look was crazy! I personally love it, however I know it is a bit bold and daring so the response that I got from it was so nice. I dare you guys to try something layered and maybe even a little weird like this. The wintertime is the perfect opportunity to try different layering techniques, so you have zero excuse not to try!!! 
I missed being here the last few weeks and I am so happy to be back. I have never been this busy in my entire life and it is truthfully so fun and definitely rewarding! There is not greater feeling than seeing your hard work and commitment pay off! Until next time!! 

Simply, 
Jacquelyn

Mixin' & Matchin'

11/17/2014

0 Comments

 
Hi everyone! I hope you all had a great weekend and found someway to enjoy this cold weather!! It seems as though fall decided to take a break and let winter take over for a bit. I am having a serious problem with this! However this weekend I had a relaxing few days back in Connecticut visiting my family. On Sunday my mom and I met my friend Erin and her mom for brunch in Bryant Park. This brunch date has become a tradition that the four of us do around this time of year. I wanted to show you guys my outfit for our brunch date and for the rest of the day! I hope you all enjoy! 
Top-Zara
Pants-H&M
Coat-Anthropologie
Shoes-Aldo (6 years ago...)
Bag-Chanel 
Brunch is one of my favorite meals because it is usually an excuse to get dressed up. Heels are also very appropriate for brunch which is another reason why I love it! This outfit is almost all made up of clothes that live in the back of my closet. I have had these pants for over 5 years and I still love when I get to wear them. This season mixing patterns is a big trend and I played with that look for brunch. I matched these blush colored lace pants with a blue and purple plaid shirt. This shirt is clearly 100% sheer so I decided to wear a maroon bralette to match the deep purples in the shirt. I also tucked the shirt in to give myself a bit more of a defined shape. 
These pants are very comfortable, as they are loose and high waisted and gradually get tighter down my legs. I tend to tuck in my tops when I wear pants like this because I like having a defined waist when wearing anything high waisted. Having a defined waist line is important if you want to slim yourself out as well. 
This shirt is the only thing that I have not had for that long. I bought this shirt about a year ago and have never worn it until this day. I love how simple it is yet is definitely a statement piece. I decided that adding a bralette under the shirt would make it day time appropriate. I got this bralette from Aerie and I highly recommend this store for undergarments. 
This coat is another oldie of mine. I got it about 4 years ago and only wore a few times until recently. I found it over the summer when I was going through my winter clothes and fell in love with it all over again. It is oversized which I love and I can wear it draped over my shoulders like I did for brunch. Adding a coat over your shoulders looks classy and effortless while still adding a bit of sexiness to the look. Not to mention it is so comfortable and warm. I chose this coat to wear because the neutral tones of the wool added a bit of dimension to the overall look. It tied all of the colors together. Since my shirt had blues in it I decided to carry my navy blue Chanel bag with me to bring out some color as well. 
I chose these shoes mostly because they were the only fall/winter heels I found in my closet since I forgot to pack heels with me from school. I then noticed that they would be perfect since they kept with the neutral tones of the outfit. The chocolate brown leather detailing on the shoe matched the dark brown buttons of my coat perfectly as well. Subtle matching details like this and the blue from my shirt and my bag, is an easy way to make an outfit cohesive. Matching too much can be dull, so a trick is to take certain colors from the outfit and match accessories with it. Try this trick sometime, I promise it will work! 
I think this shirt is too beautiful to not show without the coat on. I did take my coat off during brunch and wanted to show you guys the outfit this way as well. I love the sheerness of the shirt while still showing the pattern of the fabric. Pairing two different patterns together can be tricky and can become a hot mess very quickly. However, if you do want to match patterns try taking one subtle pattern and one bold pattern to start with. As you gradually become more confident rocking bold statements you can try for two bold patterns and you'll have a statement look! 
I hope you all enjoyed this post as much as I enjoyed the day I wore this outfit! I look forward to being back with you guys next week and I hope your week is wonderful! Bye dolls!! 
Simply,
Jacquelyn
