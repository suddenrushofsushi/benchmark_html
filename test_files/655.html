Allegiant Book Review

SPOILERS ARE INCLUDED IN THE REVIEW. IF YOU DON’T WANT THE BOOK TO BE SPOILED FOR YOU, DO NOT READ MORE. Don’t say I didn’t warn you. This isn’t really a proper review, more of my feelings towards the events in the book. Enjoy. (Written by haymitch)

I’m so heartbroken over this book I don’t even know what to think. If I hadn’t been in the public eye when I finished reading this book, I would have cried my eyes out. The ending was so gut-wrenching and heartbreaking. I don’t think I’ll ever recover.

Tris’ death made me want to hate the book. But, obviously Veronica Roth did was she thought was best for the trilogy and it’s probably the most realistic ending for the Divergent story. I didn’t expect a happy ever after ending, but it feels weird. Tris was my favourite character in the trilogy and I thought her death was unnecessary. I thought if Veronica was to kill off a main character, that it would be Tobias, not Tris. It’s so bittersweet I have no idea how to feel about it. I want to hate the book but I love the story and the characters too much.

Uriah’s death really hit me hard. I was really upset by it because he didn’t deserve to die! The way his life support being turned off was written, how Zeke and Hana held each other during Uriah’s last moments, it was so hard to read and practically killed me. The way Tobias took the responsibility of Uriah’s death onto his shoulders kind of annoyed me.. I felt like he shouldn’t have. It wasn’t his fault, and even though he was involved in what caused the accident, it was just that. An accident.

Tori Tori Tori Tori. I can’t believe Veronica killed her off! I thought from the beginning she was such an important character in the trilogy. I didn’t understand the message behind her dying, because for Tris, clearly sacrifice was what was being shown through her taking Caleb’s place and taking the chance against the death serum, and ultimately she still paid the price. Tori’s death felt like it was just something to keep that part of the book interesting. Which it didn’t. It just hurt, a lot.

I liked the dual perspectives of Tris and Tobias narrative, but at the same time I didn’t. I liked being able to know what Tobias was feeling, particularly towards his parents and Tris and Uriah’s deaths. Obviously Tris’ death probably wouldn’t have happened if Veronica had kept with just Tris’ perspective, so I guess that’s why I’m so fond of the idea of Veronica keeping it as only Tris narrating.

Bravery seemed to be a reoccurring theme throughout the series, but I felt it was extremely strong in this book. Evelyn choosing Tobias over power was a really brave decision for her, and Tris choosing to let her brother die, right up to the last possible moment, sacrificing herself. I hate myself for saying this but, I thought Caleb deserved the fate he was originally given. Sacrificing Tris over Caleb annoyed me a lot. As usual Tris jumped into Caleb’s position without a second thought, even after all he put her through. Errrrrghhhhhhh. Tris can be so annoying sometimes!

The epilogue killed me. Tobias facing his fear of heights to spread Tris’ ashes was extremely touching, but it felt weird. I don’t know if it was a Dauntless thing to do, but I thought it would have been nicer if her ashes were spread somewhere more symbolic for her.

Allegiant was anything but boring. It was heartbreaking, confusing, and extremely frustrating. I’m just skimming the surface with what I’ve written, but I’m still trying to recover from finishing the book. I don’t think I’ll ever be able to pick up any of the books again, at least not for a while. Weirdly, I couldn’t picture Shailene Woodley as Tris or Theo James as Tobias while reading, which worried me. The idea of Allegiant becoming a movie scares me. I don’t think I could handle that.

I thought it was an incredible final book to an amazing trilogy which I will always hold close to my heart. Thank you Veronica Roth, for the truly amazing characters and story you created.
