Mortal Kombat X: A Review

Ryan Shelton, Reporter • April 20, 2015 • 849 views • Leave a Comment

Ryan Shelton, the Pony Express Gaming Guru, reviews Mortal Kombat X.

Mortal Kombat X is the tenth and most recent sequel to the hit fighting game series Mortal Kombat by Ed Boon and his team NetherRealm Studios.

This game like other games in the Mortal Kombat series, this game is gruesome thanks to finishing moves called fatalities. Fatalities make this series excel over other fighting games. In this one, thanks to the amazing graphics, the series has never looked so brutal, and thanks to the excellent sound design, it sounds so real.

Among the things that I like in Mortal Kombat more than any other fighting game are its characters and their design. My favorite in this game is Erron Black, a mercenary gunslinger, and thanks to the excellent design, it perfectly shows what type of man he is. This goes for all the character like Kotal Kahn, whose design is heavily inspired by Aztec culture, which help understands the world around him.

Something Mortal Kombat X has that not many fighting games have is a story mode. Most fighting games have a ladder where you beat enemies one on one until you get to the top, but MKX has a full-fledged story, something I really like in my video games. In the story you play as some of the characters, and this help players determine their fighting style and learn the combos of the characters.

After Ive said that it sounds like this game is perfect but there is one thing that makes the game not perfect and its a biggie. Recently, games have been using something called micro-transactions, first found in free-to-playmobile games, but now games have been trying to adapt them and it usually involves taking things out of the game or dumbing the game down. In MKX , its the ladder. If youre too lazy to learn the finishing moves, you can now press two buttons and that does it automatically, and if youve read my review on Bloodborne you know exactly how I feel about this.

Another thing is there are a ton of characters already announced as downloadable content (DLC) that are already in the game! So it feels like they cut the characters straight out of the game and sold extra.

Even though all of the absurd DLC practices in this game, its still worthy of a purchase and a must-have for fighting game fan, MK fan or for someone who is just entering the series.
